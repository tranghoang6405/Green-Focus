COIS 2750H

This is a general-interest course that examines the current state of computer crime and forensic analysis. We explore the spectrum of cybercrimes in detail, considering how real-world crimes have incorporated technology, and the resultant unique forms of offending that have emerged as a direct result. We consider the emergent unique subcultures in online environments around a form of deviance, crime, or a specific ideology. We also study how law enforcement agencies investigate and enforce the laws that have been created to address these issues.

COIS 3560H

This course is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of HCI, focusing on the design, evaluation, and implementation of interactive computing systems, aiming to optimize user experiences and enhance overall usability. Students will delve into the theoretical foundations of HCI, exploring cognitive psychology, user-centred design, and social theories related to understanding users. The course will also cover the practical aspects of HCI, including usability testing, user interface (UI) design, and the integration of user feedback into the development process.
